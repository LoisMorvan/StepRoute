Privacy Policy for StepRoute

1. Introduction

StepRoute is a mobile application designed to help users generate walking routes based on a target number of steps.

This Privacy Policy explains how StepRoute accesses, uses, stores, and transmits data when you use the application. It also describes your choices and your rights.

By using StepRoute, you acknowledge that certain data may be processed as described in this Privacy Policy.

2. Identity of the developer

  • App name: StepRoute
  • Developer: Lois Morvan
  • Contact email: lois.morvan.pro@gmail.com

If you have questions about privacy or data protection relating to StepRoute, you may contact the developer using the email address above.

3. Scope of this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to the StepRoute mobile application and to data processed in connection with its core features, including:

  • route generation
  • address search
  • map display
  • optional live walking session features
  • local storage of preferences, history, and favorites

This Privacy Policy does not govern third-party services that you may access through or alongside the app, except to explain when StepRoute sends data to them in order to provide functionality.

4. Data categories processed by StepRoute

Depending on how you use the app, StepRoute may access, process, or transmit the following categories of data.

4.1 Precise location

If you choose to use your current location, StepRoute may access your device's precise location.

  • latitude and longitude derived from your device location
  • location data used as the route starting point
  • live position updates shown on the map during an active walk, if you enable that feature

4.2 Address search data

If you type an address or place name in the search field, StepRoute processes that search query and may transmit it to a geocoding service in order to return matching results.

  • the search text you type
  • the selected address result
  • the coordinates associated with the selected address

4.3 Activity and fitness-related data

If you start a live walking session, StepRoute may request permission to access Android activity recognition in order to read step count information in real time.

  • live step count during the current active session

Based on the current version of the app, live step count is used in-app during the session and is not sent to the developer's backend by the app.

4.4 Preferences and locally stored app data

StepRoute stores some information locally on your device to preserve your preferences and previously generated routes.

  • your height
  • your target step count
  • your preferred route type
  • language preference
  • theme preference
  • route options such as avoiding highways or preferring green areas
  • route history
  • favorite routes

4.5 Route generation parameters

When you generate a route, StepRoute prepares and transmits request data needed to calculate a route.

  • the selected starting coordinates
  • route type
  • target distance derived from your step goal
  • stride length derived from your height
  • route preferences

5. Data we do not intentionally collect

StepRoute does not require account creation and does not intentionally collect the following for its current version:

  • username or password
  • payment information
  • government identifiers
  • contacts
  • calendar data
  • photos or videos from your device library
  • audio recordings
  • messages
  • advertising identifiers for marketing use

StepRoute also does not provide in-app purchases in its current version.

6. How we use data

StepRoute uses data only for the operation of app features and related technical purposes.

  • generate a walking route that matches your step goal
  • identify a route starting point based on your current location or a searched address
  • display the generated route on a map
  • optionally show your position on the map during an active walk
  • count your steps during a live walking session
  • save your settings, route history, and favorites locally on your device
  • troubleshoot app issues and maintain service reliability, to the extent technically necessary

StepRoute is not intended for medical diagnosis, treatment, or emergency response.

7. Legal bases for processing

Where privacy laws such as the GDPR apply, StepRoute generally relies on one or more of the following legal bases depending on the processing activity:

  • your consent, for example when you grant location or activity recognition permissions
  • performance of a service requested by you, for example when you request route generation or address search
  • legitimate interests in operating, securing, and improving the app, where applicable and permitted by law
  • compliance with legal obligations, where required by applicable law

8. Permissions requested by the app

8.1 Location permission

StepRoute may request foreground location access when you choose to use your current location as a route starting point or when you use route-following functionality that depends on your live position.

You may refuse this permission and use address search instead.

8.2 Activity recognition permission

StepRoute may request Android activity recognition permission when you start a live walking session.

This permission is used to count steps during the active session and display them in the app.

You may refuse this permission and still use the route generation features of the app.

9. Local storage on your device

Some data is stored locally on your device using local storage technologies, including AsyncStorage.

This local storage allows the app to remember your settings and route-related data between sessions.

  • height
  • route preferences
  • theme and language preferences
  • route history
  • favorites
  • step goal

Local storage on your device is under your control through your device settings and app removal actions.

10. When data is transmitted off your device

Some app functionality requires transmitting data off your device to external services.

10.1 Route generation

When you generate a route, StepRoute sends route request data to the developer-operated routing endpoint:

  • https://steproute.lois-morvan.workers.dev

That endpoint processes the request and forwards it to the routing service used by the app.

  • selected starting coordinates
  • route type
  • target distance
  • stride length
  • route preferences

10.2 Address search and suggestions

When you search for an address, StepRoute sends your query to the geocoding service used by the app:

  • https://nominatim.openstreetmap.org/search
  • the text of your search query
  • standard technical request metadata typically transmitted during web requests

10.3 Opening a route in Google Maps

If you tap "Open in Maps", StepRoute creates a directions URL and opens it in Google Maps or a web browser.

This may transmit route data, including route coordinates, to Google when you choose to use that feature.

11. Third-party services

StepRoute relies on external services to provide core functionality. Those third parties may process data under their own privacy policies and terms.

  • Cloudflare Workers, used as the developer-operated route request endpoint
  • the routing provider used behind that endpoint
  • Nominatim / OpenStreetMap, used for address search
  • Google Maps, if you explicitly choose to open a route there
  • map tile providers used to display map data in the app

You should review the privacy policies and terms of those services where appropriate.

12. Data sharing, sale, and advertising

StepRoute does not sell personal data.

StepRoute does not share personal data with third parties for targeted advertising or marketing purposes.

However, StepRoute may transmit data to service providers or external services when necessary to provide functionality requested by you, such as route generation, address search, map rendering, and opening a route in Google Maps.

Such transmissions are functional in nature and are part of the technical operation of the app.

13. Analytics and advertising

Based on the current version of the app:

  • StepRoute does not use advertising SDKs
  • StepRoute does not use third-party analytics SDKs for advertising profiling
  • StepRoute does not require a user account

If analytics, crash reporting, or advertising features are added in the future, this Privacy Policy may be updated.

14. Data retention

14.1 Data stored locally on your device

Locally stored app data remains on your device until:

  • you delete app data through your device settings
  • you uninstall the app
  • or the data is overwritten during normal app use, such as history replacement

14.2 Data transmitted to external services

Data sent to external services may be retained according to the practices and privacy policies of those services.

Because StepRoute relies on third-party infrastructure for some functionality, retention periods for externally processed data may not be fully controlled by the developer.

15. Security

StepRoute uses network requests over HTTPS for app communications in the current version of the app.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

While reasonable efforts may be used to protect data processed by the app and its supporting services, absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

16. International data transfers

Depending on your location and the infrastructure used by third-party services, data processed through StepRoute may be transmitted to or processed in countries other than your own.

By using the app, you understand that data may be processed by infrastructure or service providers located in multiple jurisdictions.

17. Children's privacy

StepRoute is not specifically directed to children under 13.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has provided personal data in a manner that raises a privacy concern, you may contact the developer.

18. Your choices

You can choose whether to use certain features that involve data processing.

  • you may choose not to grant location permission
  • you may choose not to grant activity recognition permission
  • you may choose not to use address search
  • you may choose not to open a route in Google Maps

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights regarding personal data, including rights to:
